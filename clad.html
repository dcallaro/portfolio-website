CLAD

A mobile application for classified ads

Role

UI Designer

Timeframe

June 2020

Tools

Overview

CLAD is a mobile application for searching through the crowded and often messy world of classified advertisements. From jobs and communal housing to electronics and furniture, CLAD will help you find exactly what you’re looking for.

The Problem

While modern day technology continues to advance, platforms for classified advertisements look as though they’ve been left behind in the pre-digital era. They are visually dull, outdated and require users to dig for the listings they want.

The Solution

To provide users with a modern and user-friendly platform for classified ads, CLAD offers a variety of ad categories and advanced filtering functions, allowing users to quickly find the best results for their needs and easily add their own listings.

Design Process

After conducting an in-depth competitor analysis to understand the key functionalities and pain points of current available apps on the market, I began the wireframing process with low-fidelity sketches for various user flows.

Style Guide

Design Iterations

As this was the first project I completed in my UI design course, I was less familiar with how certain designs would translate onto mobile screens. My initial design was inspired by the original classified ads found in newspapers, however this idea left my screens feeling rudimentary and old school.

I revisited my style guide and logo to come up with a new design concept that was more consistent with the original brand emotion I imagined: approachable, modern and playful.

To stay consistent with the new brand direction, I updated the location sharing screen by replacing the graphic with a bright icon and the same background design.

The icons I originally designed to indicate the various listing categories felt flat and unrelatable to users looking for real items, so I chose to replace the icons with real images.

To better organize the individual listings, I’ve grouped the content into larger categories to make for a more pleasurable user experience.

I also updated the filter icon because in prior user testing, users were more familiar with and interacted more with the updated toggle version of the icon.

Final Designs

Product Page

Allows users to view listing details and images, along with information on the listing owner and a call to action button to contact them if interested.

Filtered Search

View curated listings based on users preferences and apply additional filters to further narrow a search.

User Profiles

To save time and energy, users only need to create a profile when adding a listing or wanting to contact a seller.

Add A Listing

Users simply pick which category their item falls under and share additional details to add a new listing.

Retrospective

I’ve taken away two major key learnings having completed this project. The first is to build out the initial low-fidelity sketches as much as possible, and make multiple iterations of them to explore various design solutions before moving onto more concrete wireframes. This will hopefully save time in the long run when designing the high-fidelity screens.

The second is to nail down the visual brand and style guide before moving forward to high-fidelity wireframes to again save time and allow for a more cohesive set of designed screens.

In the next iteration of this project, I’d like to expand on the user flows and designed wireframes, adding screens for the notification center and what the process of contacting a seller would look like. I’d also like to build an interactive prototype to showcase the designs in one live product.
